# Lesson 11 (Elite Track)

## Program Stage
- Year: 10 (accelerated stream)
- Term: 1
- Week: 4
- Session: Lesson 11
- Duration: 100 minutes

## Lesson Title
Introductory Integration and Area Modelling

## Lesson Purpose
Build first-principles understanding of antiderivatives and area accumulation to support later applications.

## Learning Intentions
By the end of this lesson, the student will:
1. Reverse basic differentiation rules.
2. Compute indefinite and definite integrals of simple polynomials.
3. Interpret area under curves in context.
4. Link derivative and integral ideas conceptually.

## Success Criteria
- Core set: `>= 85%`
- Challenge set: `>= 70%`
- Timed section complete with notation control
- Area answers include correct units/context sentence

---

## Minute-by-Minute Flow (100 min)

### 0-10 min: Retrieval Warm-Up
1. Differentiate \(x^3\)
2. Differentiate \(4x^2-7\)
3. Antiderivative of \(6x\)
4. Evaluate \(\int_0^2 x\,dx\)
5. Define constant of integration

### 10-30 min: Explicit Teaching
- Antiderivative power rule.
- Constant of integration \(+C\).
- Definite integral as signed area.
- Fundamental link: derivative-integral inverse behavior.

### 30-55 min: Guided Core Set
1. Find \(\int (6x^2-4x+3)\,dx\).
2. Evaluate \(\int_1^3 (2x+1)\,dx\).
3. Evaluate \(\int_0^4 (x^2)\,dx\).
4. If \(v(t)=3t^2-2t\), find displacement from \(t=0\) to \(t=2\).
5. Find area bounded by \(y=x+2\), x-axis, and \(x=0\) to \(x=3\).
6. Check by differentiation: \(F(x)=\dfrac{3}{2}x^2-4x+C\).

### 55-70 min: Singapore-Style Non-Routine Block
1. Show two different antiderivatives of same function differ by constant.
2. Evaluate \(\int_0^a x\,dx\) and interpret geometrically.
3. Compare \(\int_{-2}^2 x\,dx\) and \(\int_{-2}^2 |x|\,dx\).

### 70-85 min: Timed ATAR-Style Mini Test
1. Find \(\int (5x^4-2x+1)\,dx\).
2. Evaluate \(\int_2^5 (3x-4)\,dx\).
3. Find area under \(y=2x\) from \(x=0\) to \(x=6\).

### 85-100 min: Exit Ticket
1. \(\int 8x\,dx\)
2. \(\int_0^3 (x+1)\,dx\)
3. One sentence: why definite integrals can be negative.

## Core Solutions
1. \(2x^3-2x^2+3x+C\)
2. \([x^2+x]_1^3=(9+3)-(1+1)=10\)
3. \([\dfrac{x^3}{3}]_0^4=\dfrac{64}{3}\)
4. \(\int_0^2(3t^2-2t)dt=[t^3-t^2]_0^2=8-4=4\)
5. \(\int_0^3(x+2)dx=[\dfrac{x^2}{2}+2x]_0^3=\dfrac{9}{2}+6=\dfrac{21}{2}\)
6. \(F'(x)=3x-4\)

## Timed Test Solutions
1. \(x^5-x^2+x+C\)
2. \([\dfrac{3x^2}{2}-4x]_2^5=\dfrac{39}{2}\)
3. Triangle area \(\dfrac12(6)(12)=36\)

## Exit Ticket Answers
1. \(4x^2+C\)
2. \([\dfrac{x^2}{2}+x]_0^3=\dfrac{15}{2}\)
3. Signed area below x-axis contributes negatively.
