# Lesson 06 (Elite Track)

## Program Stage
- Year: 10 (accelerated stream)
- Term: 1
- Week: 2
- Session: Lesson 6
- Duration: 100 minutes

## Lesson Title
Coordinate Geometry: Lines, Distance, Midpoint, and Locus Precision

## Lesson Purpose
Secure high-speed coordinate reasoning to support graph modelling, calculus geometry, and vector transition.

## Learning Intentions
By the end of this lesson, the student will:
1. Use slope-intercept-point forms interchangeably.
2. Compute distance and midpoint with reliable algebraic control.
3. Derive equations of lines under geometric constraints.
4. Solve locus-style coordinate problems with full reasoning.

## Success Criteria
- Core set: `>= 85%`
- Challenge set: `>= 70%`
- Timed section complete with <=1 algebra slip
- Every geometry claim supported by equation evidence

## Minute-by-Minute Flow (100 min)

### 0-10 min: Retrieval Warm-Up
1. Gradient between \((-2,3)\) and \((4,-9)\)
2. Midpoint of \((1,7)\), \((9,-3)\)
3. Equation through \((2,5)\) with slope \(-3\)
4. Distance between \((0,0)\) and \((6,8)\)
5. Condition for perpendicular lines

### 10-30 min: Explicit Teaching
- Slope as rate and geometric invariant.
- Parallel/perpendicular relationships.
- Point-slope form to standard form conversion.
- Locus from distance constraints and equal-distance sets.

### 30-55 min: Guided Core Set
1. Find equation of line through \((3,-2)\) and \((7,6)\).
2. Find equation of line perpendicular to \(2x-3y+5=0\) through \((1,4)\).
3. Determine if points \(A(1,2),B(4,8),C(7,14)\) are collinear.
4. Find coordinates of point dividing segment from \((2,1)\) to \((8,13)\) in ratio 1:2.
5. Find equation of perpendicular bisector of segment joining \((2,-1)\), \((6,7)\).
6. Find locus of points equidistant from \(P(2,3)\) and \(Q(8,-1)\).

### 55-70 min: Singapore-Style Non-Routine Block
1. Show triangle with vertices \((0,0),(a,0),(0,b)\) is right-angled and derive area.
2. Prove midpoint theorem using coordinate method.
3. Find all points on line \(y=2x+1\) at distance 5 from origin.

### 70-85 min: Timed ATAR-Style Mini Test
1. Equation of line through \((4,-3)\) parallel to \(3x+2y-7=0\).
2. Find distance from \((5,1)\) to \((-1,-7)\).
3. Find equation of line joining midpoint of \((2,4),(10,-2)\) to \((6,6)\).

### 85-100 min: Exit Ticket
1. Gradient of line \(5x-2y+9=0\).
2. Equation through \((0,-2)\) perpendicular to \(y=\dfrac12x+3\).
3. One sentence: how to test collinearity quickly.

---

## Core Solutions
1. Gradient \(m=2\), equation \(y=2x-8\)
2. Original slope \(\dfrac{2}{3}\), perpendicular slope \(-\dfrac{3}{2}\), equation \(y-4=-\dfrac32(x-1)\)
3. All slopes equal 2, so collinear.
4. Internal division gives \((4,5)\)
5. Midpoint \((4,3)\), segment slope 2, perpendicular slope \(-\dfrac12\), equation \(y-3=-\dfrac12(x-4)\)
6. Perpendicular bisector of \(PQ\): midpoint \((5,1)\), slope of \(PQ=-\dfrac{2}{3}\), perpendicular slope \(\dfrac32\)

## Timed Test Solutions
1. Parallel form \(3x+2y+c=0\), substitute \((4,-3)\): \(12-6+c=0\Rightarrow c=-6\), so \(3x+2y-6=0\)
2. Distance \(\sqrt{(6)^2+(8)^2}=10\)
3. Midpoint \((6,1)\), line through \((6,1)\), \((6,6)\) is \(x=6\)

## Exit Ticket Answers
1. \(y=\dfrac52x+\dfrac92\), gradient \(\dfrac52\)
2. Perpendicular slope \(-2\), equation \(y=-2x-2\)
3. Compare gradients between consecutive pairs.
