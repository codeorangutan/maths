# Lesson 01 (Elite Track)

## Program Stage
- Year: 10 (accelerated stream)
- Term: 1
- Week: 1
- Session: Lesson 1
- Duration: 100 minutes

## Lesson Title
Baseline Diagnostic + Elite Learning System Setup

## Lesson Purpose
Establish a precise starting profile for an exceptional student targeting top-100 state placement, and set the performance operating standards used for all future lessons.

## Learning Intentions
By the end of this lesson, the student will:
1. Demonstrate current performance across core strands under timed conditions.
2. Identify strongest and weakest strands using evidence.
3. Commit to an elite study protocol (error log, verification, pacing, justification quality).

## Success Criteria
- Diagnostic completion: `100%` attempted.
- Accuracy target today: establish baseline (no fixed threshold).
- Reflection quality: at least 2 strengths, 2 gaps, and 3 specific action commitments.
- Written work standard: complete lines of reasoning for all non-routine items.

---

## Minute-by-Minute Flow (100 min)

### 0-8 min: Orientation
- Explain pathway: Years 10-12 to top-100 target.
- Clarify standards:
  - no skipped working
  - exact notation
  - proof statements require full justification
- Introduce error categories (`C`, `P`, `A`, `R`, `T`).

### 8-58 min: Baseline Diagnostic (50 min)
- Section A (No calculator): 24 marks
- Section B (Calculator allowed): 26 marks
- Total: 50 marks

### 58-73 min: Rapid Mark + Feedback Conference (15 min)
- Mark with rubric by strand.
- Build immediate strand profile.

### 73-90 min: Goal and System Design (17 min)
- Set short-cycle targets (2 weeks).
- Build personal error log template.
- Define weekly routine and verification checklist.

### 90-100 min: Exit Ticket + Commitments (10 min)
- Short technical check + reflective commitment.

## Diagnostic Paper (50 marks)

## Section A: Fluency + Structure (No Calculator, 24 marks)

1. Simplify fully: \(\dfrac{3^{-2}+2^{-1}}{5^{-1}}\). (2)
2. Rationalize: \(\dfrac{4}{\sqrt{3}-1}\). (2)
3. Expand and simplify: \((3x-2)^2-(x+1)(x-1)\). (3)
4. Factorize completely: \(2x^3-8x\). (2)
5. Solve: \(|2x-5|<7\). (2)
6. Solve inequality: \(\dfrac{x-3}{x+2}\ge 0\). (3)
7. If \(f(x)=x^2-3x+1\), find \(f(x+2)-f(x)\). (3)
8. Show \(n^2+n\) is always even for integer \(n\). (3)
9. Solve exactly: \(x+\dfrac{1}{x}=4\), \(x\neq0\). (4)

## Section B: Application + Reasoning (Calculator Allowed, 26 marks)

10. A function is defined by \(g(x)=2^{x-1}+3\).
   - a) Find \(g(4)\). (1)
   - b) Solve \(g(x)=19\). (2)
   - c) State the horizontal asymptote. (1)

11. A quadratic \(y=ax^2+bx+c\) has roots 2 and -3 and passes through \((1,-8)\).
   - Find \(a,b,c\). (4)

12. In triangle \(ABC\), \(a=8\), \(b=11\), \(C=47^\circ\).
   - a) Find side \(c\). (2)
   - b) Find area of triangle \(ABC\). (2)

13. A bag contains 5 red, 4 blue, 3 green counters. Two counters are drawn without replacement.
   - a) Probability both are red. (2)
   - b) Probability exactly one is blue. (3)

14. Let \(p(x)=x^3-6x^2+mx+12\). Given \(x=2\) is a root:
   - a) Find \(m\). (2)
   - b) Factorize \(p(x)\) fully. (3)

15. Prove or disprove with justification:
   If \(a>b\), then \(a^3>b^3\) for all real numbers \(a,b\). (4)

## Marking Key

1. \(\dfrac{1/9+1/2}{1/5}=\dfrac{11/18}{1/5}=\dfrac{55}{18}\)
2. \(\dfrac{4(\sqrt3+1)}{2}=2\sqrt3+2\)
3. \(9x^2-12x+4-(x^2-1)=8x^2-12x+5\)
4. \(2x(x^2-4)=2x(x-2)(x+2)\)
5. \(-7<2x-5<7\Rightarrow -1<x<6\)
6. Solution: \(( -\infty,-2)\cup[3,\infty)\)
7. \((x+2)^2-3(x+2)+1-(x^2-3x+1)=4x-2\)
8. \(n^2+n=n(n+1)\), product of consecutive integers, one even.
9. \(x^2-4x+1=0\Rightarrow x=2\pm\sqrt3\)
10a. 11, 10b. \(2^{x-1}=16\Rightarrow x=5\), 10c. \(y=3\)
11. \(y=a(x-2)(x+3)\), use \((1,-8)\): \(a(-1)(4)=-8\Rightarrow a=2\), so \(y=2x^2+2x-12\)
12a. \(c^2=8^2+11^2-2(8)(11)\cos47^\circ\), \(c\approx8.19\)
12b. Area \(=\frac12ab\sin C=44\sin47^\circ\approx32.18\)
13a. \((5/12)(4/11)=5/33\)
13b. Exactly one blue: \((4/12)(8/11)+(8/12)(4/11)=64/132=16/33\)
14a. \(8-24+2m+12=0\Rightarrow m=2\)
14b. \(p(x)=x^3-6x^2+2x+12=(x-2)(x^2-4x-6)\)
15. True for all reals; cubic function is strictly increasing.

- Number/Algebra (`NA`): Q1-9, 14

## Exit Ticket (10 min)

1. Solve: \(\frac{x^2-1}{x-1}\) for \(x\neq1\). (1)
2. Solve: \(|3x+2|\ge 5\). (2)
3. Prove \(n(n-1)\) is even for integer \(n\). (2)
4. One specific behavior change from today: ____ (1)

Answers:
1. \(x+1\)
2. \(x\ge1\) or \(x\le-\frac{7}{3}\)
3. Consecutive integers include one even.
