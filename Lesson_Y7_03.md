## Part 1: Ratios

### What is a Ratio?
A ratio compares two or more quantities of the same kind. The ratio \(a : b\) means for every \(a\) of one quantity, there are \(b\) of another.

**Key properties:**
- Ratios have no units (the units cancel)
- A ratio can be simplified exactly like a fraction — divide all parts by their HCF
- Order matters: \(3 : 5 \neq 5 : 3\)

**Worked Example 1:** Simplify the ratio \(24 : 36\).

HCF of 24 and 36 is 12:
\[24 : 36 = 2 : 3\]

**Worked Example 2:** Express \(45\) minutes to \(2\) hours as a ratio in simplest form.

Convert to same units — minutes:
\[45 : 120 = 3 : 8\]

---

### Dividing a Quantity in a Given Ratio

**Method:**
1. Find total number of parts
2. Find value of one part
3. Multiply for each share

**Worked Example 3:** Divide \$420 in the ratio \(3 : 4\).

Total parts: \(3 + 4 = 7\)
One part: \(\$420 \div 7 = \$60\)
Shares: \(3 \times \$60 = \$180\) and \(4 \times \$60 = \$240\)

**Check:** \(\$180 + \$240 = \$420\) ✓

**Worked Example 4:** Three friends invest in a business in the ratio \(2 : 3 : 5\). The total profit is \$8\,000. How much does each person receive?

Total parts: \(2 + 3 + 5 = 10\)
One part: \(\$8000 \div 10 = \$800\)
Shares: \(\$1600\), \(\$2400\), \(\$4000\)

---

### Equivalent Ratios and Scaling

Ratios scale like fractions. If \(a : b = 3 : 5\) and \(a = 12\), find \(b\):
\[\frac{a}{b} = \frac{3}{5} \Rightarrow \frac{12}{b} = \frac{3}{5} \Rightarrow b = \frac{12 \times 5}{3} = 20\]

**Worked Example 5:** A recipe for 4 people uses 300 g of flour. How much flour is needed for 10 people?

Scale factor: \(\dfrac{10}{4} = 2.5\)
Flour: \(300 \times 2.5 = 750\) g

## Part 2: Rates

### What is a Rate?
A rate compares two quantities of **different** kinds. Unlike ratios, rates keep their units.

Common rates:
- Speed: km/h or m/s
- Price: \$/kg or \$/L
- Flow: L/min

**Worked Example 6:** A car travels 360 km in 4.5 hours. Find its average speed.

\[\text{Speed} = \frac{\text{distance}}{\text{time}} = \frac{360}{4.5} = 80 \text{ km/h}\]

**Worked Example 7:** Which is better value — 2 kg of coffee for \$13.50 or 750 g for \$4.80?

Unit price for option A: \(\$13.50 \div 2 = \$6.75\) per kg
Unit price for option B: \(\$4.80 \div 0.75 = \$6.40\) per kg

Option B is better value.

---

### Speed, Distance, Time Triangle

\[\text{Speed} = \frac{d}{t}, \quad \text{Distance} = s \times t, \quad \text{Time} = \frac{d}{s}\]

**Worked Example 8:** A cyclist rides at 24 km/h. How far does she travel in 1 hour 45 minutes?

Convert time: \(1\dfrac{3}{4}\) hours \(= 1.75\) hours
\[d = 24 \times 1.75 = 42 \text{ km}\]

**Worked Example 9:** Two trains start from the same station. Train A travels east at 80 km/h. Train B travels west at 60 km/h. After how many hours are they 350 km apart?

They move in opposite directions, so their speeds add:
\[\text{Combined speed} = 80 + 60 = 140 \text{ km/h}\]
\[t = \frac{350}{140} = 2.5 \text{ hours}\]

## Part 3: Proportional Reasoning

### Direct Proportion
Two quantities are in **direct proportion** if when one doubles, so does the other. We write \(y \propto x\), or \(y = kx\) for some constant \(k\).

**Worked Example 10:** \(y\) is directly proportional to \(x\). When \(x = 4\), \(y = 10\). Find \(y\) when \(x = 14\).

Find \(k\): \(k = \dfrac{y}{x} = \dfrac{10}{4} = 2.5\)
\[y = 2.5 \times 14 = 35\]

### Inverse Proportion
Two quantities are in **inverse proportion** if when one doubles, the other halves. We write \(y \propto \dfrac{1}{x}\), or \(xy = k\).

**Worked Example 11:** 6 workers take 8 days to complete a job. How long would 9 workers take (assuming same rate)?

\(k = 6 \times 8 = 48\)
\[\text{Time} = \frac{48}{9} = 5\frac{1}{3} \text{ days}\]

## Part 4: Guided Practice Set

1. Simplify the ratio \(45 : 75\).
2. Express 40 minutes to 3 hours as a ratio in simplest form.
3. Divide 560 in the ratio \(3 : 5\).
4. Divide \$720 among three people in the ratio \(1 : 2 : 3\).
5. A map has a scale of \(1 : 25\,000\). A road is 7.5 cm on the map. What is its actual length in km?
6. A car uses 45 L of fuel to travel 630 km. Find its fuel consumption rate in L/100 km.
7. \(y\) is directly proportional to \(x\). When \(x = 5\), \(y = 12\). Find \(y\) when \(x = 20\).
8. 4 taps fill a tank in 15 hours. How long would 6 taps take?
9. A hiker walks 18 km in 4.5 hours. At this rate, how long (in hours and minutes) to walk 27 km?
10. Orange juice is made by mixing concentrate and water in the ratio \(2 : 7\). How much concentrate is needed for 1.8 L of juice?

## Part 5: Non-Routine Problem Block

1. **Ratio puzzle:** The ratio of boys to girls in a class is \(3 : 4\). If 5 more boys join, the ratio becomes \(5 : 4\). How many girls are in the class?

2. **Meeting problem:** Two cyclists start at opposite ends of a 120 km route at the same time. Cyclist A rides at 24 km/h and Cyclist B rides at 16 km/h. Where (distance from A's start) and when do they meet?

3. **Proportional chain:** A recipe calls for flour and sugar in the ratio \(5 : 2\). The recipe also calls for flour and butter in the ratio \(10 : 3\). What is the ratio of sugar to butter?

4. **Best value investigation:** Three options for buying rice:
   - 1 kg bag for \$2.40
   - 2.5 kg bag for \$5.50
   - 5 kg bag for \$10.80
   
   Rank by value. Under what circumstances might the cheapest per kg option not be the best choice?

## Exit Ticket

1. A train travels from City A to City B at 90 km/h, then returns at 60 km/h. Show that the average speed for the whole trip is **not** 75 km/h. Calculate the correct average speed. (Hint: use a specific distance to make it concrete.)

2. Two quantities \(P\) and \(Q\) are such that \(P\) is directly proportional to \(Q^2\). When \(Q = 3\), \(P = 36\). Find \(P\) when \(Q = 5\). What type of proportionality is this called?
