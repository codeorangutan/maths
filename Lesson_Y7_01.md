## Part 1: Integers and the Number Line

### What is an Integer?
Integers are the set of whole numbers and their negatives:
\[\ldots, -4, -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, \ldots\]

The number line is the foundation. Every integer has:
- A **position** (where it sits relative to zero)
- A **sign** (positive or negative)
- A **magnitude** (how far from zero — also called absolute value)

**Absolute value:** \(|{-5}| = 5\), \(|3| = 3\), \(|0| = 0\). The absolute value is always non-negative.

---

### Adding and Subtracting Integers

**Rule:** Adding a negative is the same as subtracting. Subtracting a negative is the same as adding.

\[5 + (-3) = 5 - 3 = 2\]
\[5 - (-3) = 5 + 3 = 8\]

**Think of it on the number line:**
- Adding a positive → move right
- Adding a negative → move left
- Subtracting a negative → flip direction → move right

**Worked Example 1:** Calculate \(-4 + 7\).

Start at \(-4\), move 7 steps right: land on \(3\).
\[-4 + 7 = 3\]

**Worked Example 2:** Calculate \(-3 - (-5)\).

Subtracting a negative flips to addition:
\[-3 - (-5) = -3 + 5 = 2\]

---

### Multiplying and Dividing Integers

**Sign rules — memorise these:**

| Operation | Signs | Result |
|---|---|---|
| \((+) \times (+)\) | Same | Positive |
| \((-) \times (-)\) | Same | Positive |
| \((+) \times (-)\) | Different | Negative |
| \((-) \times (+)\) | Different | Negative |

The same rules apply for division.

**Worked Example 3:** Calculate \((-3) \times (-4)\).

Both negative → result is positive:
\[(-3) \times (-4) = +12\]

**Worked Example 4:** Calculate \((-24) \div 6\).

Different signs → result is negative:
\[(-24) \div 6 = -4\]

**Worked Example 5:** Calculate \((-2) \times 3 \times (-5)\).

Work left to right:
\[(-2) \times 3 = -6\]
\[(-6) \times (-5) = +30\]

## Part 2: Order of Operations (BODMAS/BIDMAS)

When an expression contains multiple operations, the order you compute them matters enormously.

**BODMAS:**
1. **B**rackets first
2. **O**rders (powers and roots)
3. **D**ivision and **M**ultiplication (left to right)
4. **A**ddition and **S**ubtraction (left to right)

> Division and multiplication have equal priority — work left to right. Same for addition and subtraction.

**Worked Example 6:** Evaluate \(3 + 4 \times 2\).

Multiplication before addition:
\[3 + 4 \times 2 = 3 + 8 = 11\]
*(Not* \(7 \times 2 = 14\). That is wrong.)*

**Worked Example 7:** Evaluate \((3 + 4) \times 2\).

Brackets first:
\[(3 + 4) \times 2 = 7 \times 2 = 14\]

**Worked Example 8:** Evaluate \(20 - 4 \div 2 + 3^2\).

Step by step:
\[= 20 - 4 \div 2 + 9 \quad \text{(powers first: } 3^2 = 9\text{)}\]
\[= 20 - 2 + 9 \quad \text{(division: } 4 \div 2 = 2\text{)}\]
\[= 27 \quad \text{(left to right addition/subtraction)}\]

**Worked Example 9:** Evaluate \((-2)^3 + 5 \times (1 - 4)\).

Step by step:
\[= (-2)^3 + 5 \times (-3) \quad \text{(brackets first)}\]
\[= -8 + 5 \times (-3) \quad \text{(order: } (-2)^3 = -8\text{)}\]
\[= -8 + (-15) \quad \text{(multiplication)}\]
\[= -23\]

**Common traps:**
- \(-3^2 = -(3^2) = -9\), but \((-3)^2 = +9\). The brackets change everything.
- Division before subtraction: \(10 - 6 \div 2 = 10 - 3 = 7\), not \(4 \div 2 = 2\).

## Part 3: Guided Practice Set

Work through each problem step by step. Show every line.

1. Calculate \((-8) + 3\).
2. Calculate \(7 - (-4)\).
3. Calculate \((-6) \times (-3)\).
4. Calculate \((-20) \div (-5)\).
5. Evaluate \(3 + 6 \times 2 - 1\).
6. Evaluate \(24 \div (8 - 2) + 3^2\).
7. Evaluate \((-3)^2 - 4 \times 2\).
8. Evaluate \(5 \times (2 - 7) + (-3)^2\).
9. A submarine is at \(-40\) m. It rises 15 m, then descends 8 m. What is its final depth?
10. The temperature at midnight is \(-6°C\). By noon it has risen 13°C. Then it drops 5°C by evening. What is the evening temperature?

## Part 4: Non-Routine Problem Block

These problems require you to think carefully — not just apply a formula.

1. **Missing signs:** Place \(+\) or \(-\) in the boxes to make this true:
\[(-3) \,\square\, (-5) \,\square\, (-2) = -6\]

2. **Always, sometimes, never:** Is \(a - b = b - a\) always, sometimes, or never true for integers? Justify with examples and a general argument.

3. **Pattern problem:** The sequence \(-15, -9, -3, 3, 9, \ldots\) — find the 10th term. What is the first positive term?

4. **Error hunt:** A student writes: \(3 + 4^2 \times 2 = 7^2 \times 2 = 49 \times 2 = 98\). Find every error and write the correct solution.
