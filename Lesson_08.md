# Lesson 08 (Elite Track)

## Program Stage
- Year: 10 (accelerated stream)
- Term: 1
- Week: 3
- Session: Lesson 8
- Duration: 100 minutes

## Lesson Title
Probability Foundations: Counting, Conditional Logic, and Tree Modelling

## Lesson Purpose
Build probability fluency for ATAR-style interpretation and combinatorial reasoning.

## Learning Intentions
By the end of this lesson, the student will:
1. Calculate single and compound event probabilities accurately.
2. Distinguish independent vs dependent events.
3. Use tree diagrams and conditional probability logic.
4. Explain probability decisions in precise language.

## Success Criteria
- Core set: `>= 85%`
- Challenge set: `>= 70%`
- Timed section completed with full working
- All conditional statements justified with notation

---

## Minute-by-Minute Flow (100 min)

### 0-10 min: Retrieval Warm-Up
1. \(P(\text{even on die})\)
2. \(P(\text{not red})\) if bag has 3 red, 5 blue
3. Mean of 4, 6, 10
4. Define independent events
5. Quick fraction simplification drill

### 10-30 min: Explicit Teaching
- Sample space design and counting.
- With/without replacement models.
- Conditional probability \(P(A|B)=\dfrac{P(A\cap B)}{P(B)}\).
- Tree diagrams as structured reasoning.

### 30-55 min: Guided Core Set
1. Two coins tossed: find \(P(\text{exactly one head})\).
2. Bag with 5 red, 4 blue, 3 green. Two draws without replacement: \(P(\text{both red})\).
3. Same bag: \(P(\text{exactly one blue})\).
4. In class, 12 students do Methods, 9 Specialist, 5 both, total 25. Find \(P(M\cup S)\).
5. If \(P(A)=0.4, P(B)=0.5, P(A\cap B)=0.2\), find \(P(A|B)\).
6. Decide whether A and B are independent from Question 5.

### 55-70 min: Singapore-Style Non-Routine Block
1. Find probability sum of two dice is prime.
2. Prove \(P(A|B)\neq P(B|A)\) in general by example.
3. Three-question true/false random guess: probability of at least two correct.

### 70-85 min: Timed ATAR-Style Mini Test
1. Two cards from standard deck without replacement: \(P(\text{both face cards})\).
2. Given \(P(A)=0.7, P(B)=0.6, P(A\cup B)=0.9\), find \(P(A\cap B)\).
3. Given \(P(B)=0.4\), \(P(A\cap B)=0.1\), find \(P(A|B)\).

### 85-100 min: Exit Ticket
1. Is \(P(A\cap B)=P(A)P(B)\) true for independent events?
2. Compute \(P(\text{at least one head})\) for two coin tosses.
3. One sentence: when to use conditional probability.
