# Lesson 24 (Elite Track)

## Program Stage
- Year: 10 (accelerated stream)
- Term: 3
- Week: 2
- Session: Lesson 24
- Duration: 100 minutes

## Lesson Title
Term Mastery Checkpoint and Repair Cycle Launch

## Lesson Purpose
Consolidate Year 10 Term 2-3 foundations through mixed-format assessment, execute targeted repair cycles for identified weaknesses, and establish systematic protocols for sustained improvement through error analysis.

## Learning Intentions
By the end of this lesson, the student will:
1. Execute mixed-topic problems under timed conditions with accurate method selection.
2. Diagnose errors using the C-P-A-R-T taxonomy and identify root causes.
3. Design strand-specific repair actions for the next learning cycle.
4. Demonstrate improved quality of written mathematical communication.

## Success Criteria
- Core mixed set: `>= 85%`
- Challenge mixed set: `>= 70%`
- Timed section: completed on schedule with `<= 1` timing error
- Error log: complete with at least 6 corrected items using repair protocol

### 8-48 min: Mixed Core Assessment (40 min)

**Problem 1:** Solve: \(\frac{2x-1}{x+3}=4\) (Rational equations - NA)

**Problem 2:** Find the inverse of \(f(x)=\frac{3x-1}{x+2}\) and verify. (Functions - FG)

**Problem 3:** Find maximum value of \(y=-2x^2+8x-3\). (Quadratics - NA)

**Problem 4:** Solve \(\sin x = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\) for \(x\in[0^\circ,360^\circ)\). (Trig equations - TG)

**Problem 5:** Evaluate \(\int_0^3(2x^2-3)dx\). (Integration - CA)

**Problem 6:** Find equation of tangent to \(x^2+y^2=25\) at point \((3,4)\). (Circle geometry - CG)

**Problem 7:** Differentiate \(y=4x^3-6x^2+2x-1\) and find stationary points. (Calculus - CA)

**Problem 8:** Simplify \(\frac{x^3-8}{x^2-4}\) with restrictions. (Rational expressions - NA)

**Problem 9:** A farmer has 100m of fencing for rectangular paddock against a river. Find max area. (Optimisation - NA/FG)

**Problem 10:** Prove \(n^3+3n^2+2n\) is divisible by 6 for all integers \(n\). (Number theory - NA)

---

### Core Solutions

**Problem 1:**
\[2x-1=4(x+3)=4x+12\Rightarrow-2x=13\Rightarrow x=-\frac{13}{2}\]

**Problem 2:**
\[y=\frac{3x-1}{x+2}\Rightarrow yx+2y=3x-1\Rightarrow x(y-3)=-1-2y\Rightarrow x=\frac{1+2y}{3-y}\]
\[f^{-1}(x)=\frac{1+2x}{3-x},\quad x\neq 3\]

**Problem 3:**
Vertex at \(x=\frac{-8}{-4}=2\), \(y=-8+16-3=5\). Maximum is 5.

**Problem 4:**
Reference \(60^\circ\), positive sine in Q1, Q2: \(x=60^\circ, 120^\circ\).

**Problem 5:**
\[[\frac{2x^3}{3}-3x]_0^3=(18-9)-0=9\]

**Problem 6:**
Radius gradient \(\frac{4}{3}\), tangent gradient \(-\frac{3}{4}\).
Equation: \(y-4=-\frac{3}{4}(x-3)\Rightarrow 3x+4y=25\).

**Problem 7:**
\(\frac{dy}{dx}=12x^2-12x+2=2(6x^2-6x+1)=0\Rightarrow x=\frac{6\pm\sqrt{36-24}}{12}=\frac{6\pm\sqrt{12}}{12}=\frac{3\pm\sqrt{3}}{6}\).

**Problem 8:**
\[\frac{(x-2)(x^2+2x+4)}{(x-2)(x+2)}=\frac{x^2+2x+4}{x+2},\quad x\neq\pm 2\]

**Problem 9:**
Let width \(=w\), length \(=100-2w\). \(A=w(100-2w)=100w-2w^2\).
\(w=25\)m, length \(=50\)m. Max area = 1250 mÂ˛.

**Problem 10:**
\[n^3+3n^2+2n=n(n^2+3n+2)=n(n+1)(n+2)\]
Product of three consecutive integers: contains multiple of 2 and multiple of 3, hence divisible by 6.

### 48-63 min: Challenge Block (15 min)

**Challenge 1:** Solve system: \(y=\frac{1}{x}\) and \(y=2x-3\). (Systems - NA)

**Challenge 2:** Find area between \(y=x^2\) and \(y=4-x^2\). (Area - CA)

**Challenge 3:** If \(\sin x + \cos x = \frac{1}{3}\), find \(\sin(2x)\). (Trig identities - TG)

---

### Challenge Solutions

**Challenge 1:**
\[\frac{1}{x}=2x-3\Rightarrow 1=2x^2-3x\Rightarrow 2x^2-3x-1=0\]
\[x=\frac{3\pm\sqrt{17}}{4}\]
Two solutions: \((\frac{3+\sqrt{17}}{4}, \frac{\sqrt{17}-3}{2})\) and \((\frac{3-\sqrt{17}}{4}, \frac{-3-\sqrt{17}}{2})\).

**Challenge 2:**
Intersection: \(x^2=4-x^2\Rightarrow 2x^2=4\Rightarrow x=\pm\sqrt{2}\).
\[\int_{-\sqrt{2}}^{\sqrt{2}}(4-x^2-x^2)dx=\int_{-\sqrt{2}}^{\sqrt{2}}(4-2x^2)dx\]
\[[4x-\frac{2x^3}{3}]_{-\sqrt{2}}^{\sqrt{2}}=2(4\sqrt{2}-\frac{4\sqrt{2}}{3})=\frac{16\sqrt{2}}{3}\]

**Challenge 3:**
Square both sides: \(\sin^2x+2\sin x\cos x+\cos^2x=\frac{1}{9}\).
\[1+\sin(2x)=\frac{1}{9}\Rightarrow\sin(2x)=-\frac{8}{9}\]

### 63-78 min: Timed ATAR-Style Sprint (15 min)

**Sprint 1:** Find \(k\) such that \(x^2+kx+9=0\) has equal roots. (2 min)

**Sprint 2:** Evaluate \(\int_1^2(3x^2-2)dx\). (2 min)

**Sprint 3:** Solve \(\frac{x}{x-1}\ge 2\). (3 min)

**Sprint 4:** Find \((f\circ g)(x)\) where \(f(x)=\sqrt{x}, g(x)=4-x^2\). State domain. (3 min)

**Sprint 5:** Find point on \(y=x^2\) closest to \((0,3)\). (5 min)

---

### Sprint Solutions

**Sprint 1:**
\(\Delta=k^2-36=0\Rightarrow k=\pm 6\).

**Sprint 2:**
\([x^3-2x]_1^2=(8-4)-(1-2)=4+1=5\).

**Sprint 3:**
\(\frac{x}{x-1}-2\ge 0\Rightarrow\frac{x-2(x-1)}{x-1}\ge 0\Rightarrow\frac{2-x}{x-1}\ge 0\).
Solution: \(1<x\le 2\).

**Sprint 4:**
\(\sqrt{4-x^2}\), domain: \(4-x^2\ge 0\Rightarrow-2\le x\le 2\).

**Sprint 5:**
Minimize \(D^2=x^2+(x^2-3)^2=x^2+x^4-6x^2+9=x^4-5x^2+9\).
\(\frac{d(D^2)}{dx}=4x^3-10x=0\Rightarrow 2x(2x^2-5)=0\Rightarrow x=0\) or \(x=\pm\sqrt{\frac{5}{2}}\).
At \(x=0\): \(D^2=9\). At \(x=\sqrt{\frac{5}{2}}\): \(D^2=\frac{25}{4}-\frac{25}{2}+9=\frac{11}{4}\).
Closest point: \((\sqrt{\frac{5}{2}}, \frac{5}{2})\) or \((-\sqrt{\frac{5}{2}}, \frac{5}{2})\), distance \(\sqrt{\frac{11}{4}}=\frac{\sqrt{11}}{2}\).

### 78-92 min: Error Audit and Repair Design (14 min)

**Error Taxonomy (C-P-A-R-T):**
- **C**oncept: Misunderstanding definition, theorem, or principle
- **P**rocedure: Wrong method or algorithm selected
- **A**lgebra: Arithmetic or symbolic manipulation error
- **R**easoning: Logical error in argument or deduction
- **T**iming: Ran out of time; pacing issue

**Error Audit Protocol:**
1. Review each incorrect problem
2. Tag first error point by category (C, P, A, R, T)
3. Write correct solution from first wrong line
4. Identify pattern across errors (same category? same topic?)
5. Select 3 priority strands for next fortnight

**Repair Cycle Design:**
- For Concept errors: Re-read teaching notes, do worked examples
- For Procedure errors: Practice 5 same-type problems with solution check
- For Algebra errors: Drill 10 rapid calculations, verify each
- For Reasoning errors: Write full proof/justification for 3 problems
- For Timing errors: Do timed section again with strict pacing
